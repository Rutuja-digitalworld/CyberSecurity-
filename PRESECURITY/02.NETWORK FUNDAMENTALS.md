**Introduction to Networking**

*A network is a group of connected devices that can communicate and share information.
*Examples of networks in daily life: transportation systems, power grids, postal services, and social connections.
*In computing, networks connect devices such as computers, phones, servers, cameras, and IoT devices.
*A network can consist of 2 devices or billions of devices connected worldwide.
*Networks are essential for services like the internet, weather monitoring, electricity distribution, and traffic management.
*Understanding networking is important in cybersecurity because most cyber attacks and defenses involve networks.

*Quick Definition*
Networking: The process of connecting devices so they can exchange data and communicate with each other.

*Exam Tip*
Network = Connected devices that share data and resources.

Internet is made up of many small networks all joined together.  These small networks are called private networks, where networks connecting these small networks are called public networks -- or the Internet! So, to recap, a network can be one of two types:
1.A private network
2.A public network

**IP Address & MAC Address**
*Device Identification*
Just like humans have a name and fingerprints, devices have:
1.IP Address → Can change (like a name)
2.MAC Address → Permanent and unique (like fingerprints)

**IP Address**
•An IP Address (Internet Protocol Address) identifies a device on a network.
•It can change over time.
•No two devices can use the same IP address on the same network at the same time.
•IPv4 addresses are divided into 4 octets separated by dots.
Example
192.168.1.77


**Types of IP Addresses**
1. Private IP Address
•Used inside a local network (home, school, office).
•Helps devices communicate with each other.
Example:
192.168.1.77
192.168.1.74

2. Public IP Address
•Used to identify a network on the Internet.
•Provided by the ISP (Internet Service Provider).
•Multiple devices in the same home usually share the same public IP.
Example:
86.157.52.21

**IPv4 vs IPv6**
#IPv4
•Current widely used version.
•Uses 32-bit addressing.
•Supports about 4.3 billion addresses (2³²).
Example:
192.168.1.77

#IPv6
•Newer version created due to IPv4 address shortage.
•Uses 128-bit addressing.
•Supports about 340 undecillion addresses (2¹²⁸).
•More efficient and scalable.
Example:
2001:0db8:85a3:0000:0000:8a2e:0370:7334Current widely used version.

**MAC Address**
#What is a MAC Address?
•A MAC (Media Access Control) Address is a unique physical address assigned to a device's network interface card (NIC).
•It is assigned by the manufacturer during production.
•Used to identify devices on a local network.

#Format
Consists of 12 hexadecimal characters (0–9, A–F).
Written in pairs separated by colons (:)
Example:
a4:c3:f0:85:ac:2d

**MAC Spoofing**
•MAC Spoofing is the process of changing or faking a MAC address.
•A device pretends to be another device on the network.

#Why is it Dangerous?
If security systems trust a specific MAC address, an attacker can spoof that address and gain unauthorized access.
#Example
•A firewall allows only the administrator's MAC address.
•An attacker changes their MAC address to match the administrator's.
•The firewall mistakenly trusts the attacker

**Key Points**
•IP Address = Identifies where a device is on a network.
•MAC Address = Identifies which device it is.
