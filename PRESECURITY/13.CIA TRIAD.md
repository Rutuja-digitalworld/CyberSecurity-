Security does not simply mean stopping attacks or having security tools in place. In cyber security, being secure means ensuring specific conditions for the digital data. These conditions are the key aspects around which the security revolves.

1.Confidentiality

2.Integrity

3.Availability

 ## Confidentiality🔒

•**Definition:** Ensures only authorized users can access sensitive data.

•**Examples:**

•Gmail password on sticky note → ❌ No Confidentiality

•Personal document leaked online → ❌ No Confidentiality

•Company documents accessible only to required employees → ✅ Confidentiality

**How to Protect:** Encryption, Access Control, Strong Passwords

 ## Integrity ✅ 

**Definition:** Ensures data is not modified by unauthorized users.

**Examples:**

•Grade changed before submission → ❌ No Integrity

•Attendance record altered after being locked → ❌ No Integrity

•Data changed through authorized approval → ✅ Integrity

**How to Protect:** Hashing, Digital Signatures, Access Controls

## Availability 🌐

**Definition:** Ensures data and services are accessible when needed.

**Examples:**

•Company website goes offline → ❌ No Availability

•Critical service disrupted by software installation → ❌ No Availability

•Systems accessible during working hours → ✅ Availability

**How to Protect:** Backups, Redundancy, Load Balancers, DDoS Protection
